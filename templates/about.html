From Frustration to Innovation

How I, as a G11 student from Kyrgyzstan, transformed a forest research at UWC ISAK Japan. Built with love FOR students, BY students.

üå≤ The Problem in the Karuizawa Forest

You're a G11 student from Kyrgyzstan, sitting in ESS class at UWC ISAK Japan, listening to your teacher explain for the hundredth time how another group lost their field data right after the class. The beautiful Karuizawa forest surrounds your campus, with a view to the magnificent Mount Asama, but how do the students collect species data for ESS class? It's stuck in the stone age.

Students forget or lose their paper logs, and we waste time rewriting them. There's no easy way to visualize where samples were taken unless I manually plot them on a map. I want students to see patterns, not just numbers in Excel.

My ESS teacher's frustrations were echoing through every fieldwork session. Students would venture into our school's 30-hectare leased forest, conduct transect sampling, record everything on paper... and then watch it all disappear into backpack black holes or indesceptible handwriting

üí° The Idea Moment

Here's where my past coding adventures kicked in. I'd already built Minecraft plugins and created a weather app with temperature and humidity analysis. When I heard the same complaints session after session, something clicked: "Why not solve this with coding?"

This wasn't just another homework assignment. This was a real problem affecting real people in a real forest. And Shipwrecked which motivated me to start working on making this project come true.
